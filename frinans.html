Frinans

Frinans is a web app that helps young adults simplify their finances by providing a platform to handle their money by monitoring budgets, saving more to reach financial goals, and taking control of their financial life.

The Problem

During my walks of life, I have seen many people in my circle struggle in managing their finances. Most schools usually do not equip us with the basics of money management such as budgeting, saving, debt, investing, and more.
I wanted to rethink the way people could make wise financial choices for everyday living — while at the same time avoiding mistakes that will lead to lifelong money struggles, and eventually building healthy financial habits. So as part of my UX Design study, I decided to design a solution and tackle this problem.

The Solution

My solution was an app called Frinans that helps young adults simplify their finances by providing a platform to handle their money by monitoring budgets, saving more to reach financial goals, and taking control of their financial life.

Working Problem Statement

Watersport enthusiasts need an easy solution to understand local weather conditions that are typically shown in complex technical charts so they can plan a safe and enjoyable time in the water.


Competition

Weather forecasts are mainly about applying sunscreen or grabbing your umbrella.

The Competition Space
The primary weather forecasters, weather.com, noaa.gov, and weather.gov have maintained their expertise and authority by being ubiquitous. Digital and non-digital weather outlets use one or all three organization’s information and satellites. Their information to their users are not specific or shared in way that informs water play.
An analogous analysis of Glossier, glossier.com, imparts wisdom to the competition space. They are a thriving company in the health and beauty space. It is a market that large name brand companies lead, but Glossier has created a loyal and enthusiasitic community. They do this by accepting their customers will have many brands of make-up in their bags: not all complexions are the same, and their community will drive their products.

Research Context
Interviewed 5 participants with varying skill levels and favorite watersports.

Affinity Mapping: Third Cluster Session

Research Goals

What weather forecasting apps do users use before heading out into the water?
How often do they check weather apps before a trip? During the trip?
Have they ever misread a weather forecast in the past? How often? And what could be made better?

>

Top 5 Needs/Goals

I would love to add layers of information by easily toggling.
I would love a simple rating if it is a nice day to go kayaking.
If I can customize my homescreen that would be great.
Accuracy is important
Imagery is my best friend.

>

Top 5 Frustrations

Glare is a problem.
Scrolling is hard to find information.
The easiest to misread are the tides.
I exit an app quickly, if I can’t find information fast.
I don’t like reading text, I need images to understand most things.
